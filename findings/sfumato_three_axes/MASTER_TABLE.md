# Sfumato — master result table

Every headline number in the paper with exact (Clopper–Pearson) binomial
95% CI. All evaluations are GSM8K-test, N=200, single seed (SEED=0),
LLaDA-8B-Instruct at k=64 denoising steps, Qwen2.5-{0.5B, 1.5B}-Instruct
planners. Generated by `scripts/binom_ci.py` from the source repo.

| cmaj b=5 | 79% test [72.7, 84.4] | 81.5% dev [75.4, 86.6] | 79.5% test [73.2, 84.9] |

LoRA hyperparameters: r=8, α=16, lr=5e-5, 5,000 steps, p_mask ~ U(0.1, 0.5).

Trained on `eren23/sfumato-prefix-robust-gsm8k` (7,473 GSM8K-train problems
× 8 prefix tiers = 59,784 rows). Prefix tiers: none / minimal / hint / xml
/ weak Q-0.5B plan / medium Q-1.5B plan / strong Q-7B plan / oracle gold.

The v2/v3 split: v2 used the standard Llama target list
`[q_proj, k_proj, v_proj, o_proj, gate_proj, up_proj, down_proj]`; only 4 of
7 names matched LLaDA's `LLaDALlamaBlock` (which uses olmo-style FFN names
`ff_proj, up_proj, ff_out` and attention output `attn_out`). v3 retrains
with the LLaDA-correct list — full FFN coverage at 22M params vs v2's 10M.

## Track 2 — commit-LoRA (consensus distillation)

| Setup | Inference | Training | c2c | cmajc |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|
| no commit | — | — | 70.5% [63.7, 76.7] | — |
| commit v1 | last-block | answer-span | 70.5% [63.7, 76.7] | 81.5% [75.4, 86.6] |
| commit v2 | last-block | answer-span | 70.5% [63.7, 76.7] | **82.0% [76.0, 87.1]** |
| no commit (v3 base) | — | — | 73.0% [66.3, 79.0] | — |
| commit v3 | n_blocks=3 | full-response | **79.0% [72.7, 84.4]** | **82.5% [76.5, 87.5]** |

v1 had a module-name bug (only `up_proj` of `[gate_proj, up_proj, down_proj]`
matched). v2 fixed names; both stuck at 70.5%. v3 fixes two design errors
simultaneously: commit fires on sub-blocks 2–4 of 4 (not just last), and
training loss is computed over the full response span (not just answer
tail). The two errors mask each other — neither alone closes the gap.

Disentangling ablations:

| Setup | LoRA | commit | n_blocks | training loss | c2c | Δ |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---:|---:|
| Track 1 v3 alone | v3 | — | — | — | 73.0% | — |
| **ABL_A** | v3 | v2 | **3** | answer-span | **77.0% [70.5, 82.6]** | **+4.0** |
| **ABL_B** | v3 | v3 | 1 | full-response | 73.0% [66.3, 79.0] | +0.0 |
| **v3 full** | v3 | v3 | **3** | full-response | **79.0% [72.7, 84.4]** | **+6.0** |

Block coverage drives most of the lift (+4 pp). Full-response loss alone,
with last-block-only commit, does literally nothing (+0.0 pp). Together,
+6 pp. The cleanest empirical signature of the mechanism: even better
supervision cannot rescue surgery whose temporal placement is wrong.

## b=1 vs b=5 — branch aggregation absorbs the structural correction

| | b=1 (c2c) | b=5 (cmajc) |
|---|---:|---:|
| commit v2 | 70.5% [63.7, 76.7] | 82.0% [76.0, 87.1] |
| commit v3 | 79.0% [72.7, 84.4] | 82.5% [76.5, 87.5] |
| Δ (v3 − v2) | **+8.5 pp** | **+0.5 pp** |
| CI overlap | none | full |

At b=1, the structural fix is load-bearing. At b=5, stochastic
branch-vote already recovers most of the consensus quality the
late-block-only v2 misses, and v3's structural advantage has nowhere to
go. Single-pass inference is where weight-space surgery shows its real
value.

## Sampling diversity (apples-to-apples on test, b=5, t=0.7)

| Bin | Base on test | Track 1 v2 + commit v2 |
|---|---:|---:|
| 5/5 same | 51.5% [44.3, 58.6] | **47.5% [40.4, 54.7]** |
| 4/5 unique | 6.0% [3.1, 10.2] | 8.5% [5.0, 13.3] |
| 3/5 unique | 11.5% [7.4, 16.8] | **18.0% [12.9, 24.0]** |
| 2/5 unique | 27.5% [21.4, 34.2] | 19.5% [14.2, 25.7] |
| 5/5 unique | 3.5% [1.4, 7.1] | 6.5% [3.5, 10.9] |
| Mean unique answers / problem | 1.825 | **2.07** (+13%) |

Diversity expanded under the prefix-robust LoRA, not collapsed. Inverse
of the standard encoder-collapse story — the eight-tier prefix
randomization implicitly taught "many surfaces, same content" and that
lesson generalized to "many CoT trajectories, same answer" at inference.

## Self-consistency rebuttal

| Model | Setup | Accuracy |
|---|---|---:|
| Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct | AR self-consistency b=5 t=0.7 | **40.5% [33.6, 47.7]** |
| LLaDA-8B-Instruct | cmaj b=5 t=0.7 (range) | **79.0 — 82.5%** |

A 38–42 pp gap. Whatever LLaDA's branching is doing, it depends on the
diffusion sampler's specific properties — likely that within-block
uncertainty is jointly resolved across many tokens, producing genuinely
different reasoning paths rather than the AR-style temperature jitter that
mostly produces surface variation.
